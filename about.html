About

Hello, my name is Jinge Wang. Welcome to my place.

From Academia to Real-World Solutions

I am a Computer Science Ph.D. graduate from West Virginia University, where I was privileged to work under the guidance of Dr. Xin Li and Dr. Shuo Wang. Additionally, I hold a Master's degree in Statistics from WVU, equipping me with a solid foundation in both computational techniques and statistical analysis. My journey has been one of exploration across data analysis, machine learning, signal processing, and computer vision, with a focus on real-world applications in complex fields.

In my previous role as a postdoctoral fellow in Dr. Gangqing Hu's Laboratory, I delved into the fascinating intersection of artificial intelligence and bioinformatics. My work centered on creating and refining multimodal large language models designed to tackle intricate image-related tasks, a challenge that demanded both technical innovation and a deep understanding of biological data. This experience was a testament to the power of interdisciplinary collaboration, as I integrated machine learning and statistical approaches to unravel complex research questions in computational biology.

Today, I am a Data Scientist at Polygon Health Analytics (PHA), where I continue to blend my academic insights with industry demands. At PHA, I develop AI-driven and Real-World Data (RWD) solutions aimed at advancing healthcare and clinical research.
